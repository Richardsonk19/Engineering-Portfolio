Bake Function Optimization

This was the second major project I completed during my time at SharkNinja. I was responsible for creating and executing a test plan to optimize the baking performance of an oven with three heater circuits, focusing on power distribution and cycling to achieve even temperature distribution.

Project Overview:

• Bake Function Development: Created and optimized a new bake function for an oven unit with three independent heater circuits

• Temperature Control: Focused on achieving uniform temperature distribution above and below the bake tray by managing heater power distribution and duty cycling

• Control System Collaboration: Worked closely with the control systems team to design and execute tests, ensuring accurate data collection to support the development of an efficient control flow for the bake function

Electrical Engineering Development:

• Custom Test Setup: Designed and wired a custom test setup using four variacs to control voltage for three heater banks and the motor, creating a safe and efficient wiring schematic for all components

• Thermostat Control: Programmed and implemented an external digital thermostat to monitor and control power cycling for each heater circuit, optimizing heating performance

• Power Management: Managed overall system power usage, manually setting variac wattage to maximize performance while ensuring power draw stayed within the 1800W outlet limit

Internal Wiring Setup: Disconnected and rewired the oven’s internal circuits to route through individual variacs for precise power control, demonstrating electrical troubleshooting and wiring skills.


Testing and Data Collection:

• Iterative Testing: Conducted iterative tests at a fixed total power input, adjusting power ratios between heater banks to identify optimal distributions

• Control Flow Optimization: Generated data that informed control flow development, providing key insights into power cycling and heater management

• Data Analysis: Analyzed test results to evaluate effectiveness, tracking normalized temperature differences across various power ratios


Test Setup: External wiring of heaters controlled via individual variacs to optimize power distribution, with the DAQ system tracking temperatures throughout the test.




Calibration Test Analysis: Data-driven insights presented to executives, showcasing the impact of optimized power ratios on temperature uniformity, highlighting advanced data analysis and problem-solving skills.
